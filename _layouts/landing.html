Dear Future Colleague,

Seven years ago I entered the working world, stocking inventory and cleaning hermit crab cages at a beach store. My second job was making cheesesteak sandwiches. These experiences reinforced what I learned as a child from building card houses, snow forts, and Hot Wheels rollercoasters — success comes with hard work.

Since then, I have controlled research experiments on the International Space Station and founded a nonprofit using technology to support mental health on college campuses across the country. I have designed, built, and tested part or all of a 15lb BattleBot, a $10B space telescope, and a production-ready subroutine speeding fluid dynamics computations threefold for NASA, Xerox, and other companies. I have evaluated startup investments for the people who created TripAdvisor and e-readers, and I was selected by the White House to lead a team of twenty in coordinating and executing a program for the top high school students in the U.S. to meet the First Family. I graduated in June from MIT with a degree in mathematics with computer science, having also studied aerospace engineering, creative writing, and music theory. You can read more about most of these things in my résumé or a news article (or two or three).

What makes me a good employee? While I have avoided specializing in a particular field or role, I have certainly put in my 10,000 hours to become an excellent teammate — someone whom you will not regret hiring. I am a responsible and organized worker and thinker with the mindset of a certified Engineer. I get things done well yet quickly, and I compulsively target-learn key areas where I or others see deficiency. I am a strong communicator at all organization levels and for nearly any situation (e.g. formal/informal public speaking, technical/non-technical writing, board/C-suite/director/supervisor meetings, legalese, Spanish). I extend the definition of good customer service to include service toward colleagues and the organization as a whole, that we may better accomplish our mission together.

I am currently looking for a part time job (16-32 hrs per week) in San Diego. The remainder of my working time and energy will be devoted to my long-term passions for teaching and education. I am giving myself two weeks to search, ideally starting work in mid-August. Please feel free at any time to call, text, or email using the contact information provided on my résumé below. I look forward to interviewing and working with you.



Warmly,
Andy
